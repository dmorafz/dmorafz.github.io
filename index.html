Welcome

I am computational materials chemist with interests in materials for energy applications, electronics and catalysis. Also, I am passionate about the use of data science for making organisations/processes more efficient.

I obtained my Bachelor’s degree in Chemical Engineer at UJAT (Mexico) in 2011. In 2012, I moved to London to start my PhD in Computational Chemistry under the supervision of Prof Sir Richard Catlow at UCL. I received my PhD in 2016 and continued to work in collaboration with the UK Catalysis Hub at Harwell until 2017. From 2017 to 2020, I worked as Research Associate in the Department of Physics & Astronomy at UCL as part of the group of Prof Alexander Shluger. Since 2020, I am an Honorary Research Fellow at UCL. In 2020-2021, I worked as an advisor for the Secretariat of Energy Development in Tabasco, Mexico. Currently, I am the Head of the Energy and Technologies Department at Centro Municipality in Tabasco.

My main research interests can be summarised under the headings interatomic potentials development, metal oxides, surface science, catalysis, global optimisation methods, data analysis and energy efficiency. During my PhD, my particular interest was directed at the clean and defective surfaces of metal oxides and their interaction with supported nanoparticles for catalytic purposes. More recently, I have been working on modelling amorphous materials for applications in electronics and photocatalysis.

My likes include chess, travelling, coffee, reading and real ale.
